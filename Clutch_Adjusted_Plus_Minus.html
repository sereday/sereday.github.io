Clutch Adjusted Plus Minus

Every possession is not created equal. A tie game in the final minute is exponentially more important than a possession in a 30-point blowout[cite: 8]. To account for this, I developed a model to weight possessions based on the probability of them changing the outcome of the game[cite: 8].

Methodology

Using historical play-by-play data, I analyzed the frequency of comebacks from different deficits at various time intervals[cite: 8]. By calculating the difference in comeback probabilities for every 3-point swing, we can assign a "clutch weight" to every second of every game[cite: 8].

High Clutch Weight = (Time Remaining) × (Score Margin)

Applications

This data allows for a more nuanced look at player value, identifying those who truly excel when the game is on the line, as opposed to those whose raw plus-minus is padded during low-leverage minutes[cite: 8].

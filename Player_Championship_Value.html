Expected Championships Added (ECA) & MECA

Most advanced statistics approximate points and wins created or value above replacement. However, few account for the ultimate goal that teams prioritize: championships. While participating on a championship team doesn't automatically make a player the best, career value estimates are incomplete without analyzing championship impact.

Core Thesis: Expected Championships Added (ECA) and Mean Expected Championships Added (MECA) bridge the gap between points added and the probability of winning a title.

ECA is defined as the difference between the expected number of team wins (based on basketball’s Pythagorean expectation) and the expected team wins after removing the estimated effect of a player. Value for points and wins added is estimated using advanced statistical plus-minus (SPM).

The Pythagorean Relationship: Points and wins are not linearly related. A player adding +1.0 to a margin of victory has a different impact based on the team's baseline strength:

Methodology: The Beta Distribution

To determine championship expectations across different eras and league sizes, I used a beta distribution (alpha and beta ≈ 5) to model the distribution of team winning percentages. This allows for a consistent probability of winning a title given a specific win total.

The probability calculation factors in:

  • Expected conference rank/seeding.
  • Home-court advantage.
  • Dynamic playoff matchups.

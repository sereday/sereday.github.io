Estimating NBA Skill Curves

In this analysis, I build upon the work of Eli Witus to create estimated slopes that differentiate by individual player. The goal is to determine an expected Offensive Rating for each lineup based on typical usage. Since someone on the court must take a shot, low-usage lineups often have to create shots beyond their normal roles, while high-usage lineups can be more selective.

Usage and Efficiency Dynamics

Using regression analysis on lineups from 2007 to 2009, we can express the relationship between usage and efficiency. While it may seem counterintuitive, the model suggests that if a lineup takes more shots or possessions than average, its efficiency is higher than expected. This is because higher usage for a lineup often requires each individual player to reduce their personal role, leading to more selective and efficient scoring.
